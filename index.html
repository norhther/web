Secured Synthetic Mammogram Generation

Contributed to the European SECURED Project by advancing privacy-preserving synthetic data generation methods for medical imaging. Designed and implemented scalable GANs and Diffusion Models on HPC environments (Marenostrum 5), incorporating differential privacy to comply with EU regulations. Delivered validated mammogram datasets for clinical research, enhanced training datasets for radiologists, and supported cancer detection advancements. SVM Kernel Development for Omic Data

Designed and implemented novel Jaccard-Tanimoto-based kernels for Support Vector Machines (SVMs), tailored to high-dimensional and sparse omic datasets. Extended the kernel's applicability by proving its generalization to real-valued feature spaces, enabling robust handling of both binary and continuous data types. Demonstrated superior classification accuracy compared to traditional RBF kernels in tasks such as cancer subtype classification and gene expression analysis. This advancement improved the flexibility and applicability of SVMs in genomics and bioinformatics. Additionally, the approach was optimized for computational efficiency, facilitating scalable applications to large datasets in research and clinical settings.
